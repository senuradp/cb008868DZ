About Us

The National Zoo in Dehiwala Sri Lanka holds the title as of one of Asia's oldest Zoo's. This zoo currently accomodates a spectacular array of native and non-native animal species. Presently the zoo is home to a wide variety of :

  • Mammals of 80 species
  • Fish of 90 species
  • Birds of 70 species
  • Butterflies of 25 species
  • Reptiles of 25 species
  • 04 different types of Amphibian families

About Sri Lanka

Sri lanka is an island in the Indian ocean positioned within the West of the Laccadive Sea and the East of the Bay of Bengal. Sri Lanka is a beautiful island below the Southeast Coast of India and has acquired many monikers such as "THE PEARL OF THE INDIAN OCEAN", "THE TEAR DROP OF THE INDIAN OCEAN" due to its shape of the island, mesmering landscapes, its cultural richness and its wealthy civilization.

The island covers a total area of 65,610 kmÂ², Sri lanka is a also multi-cultural country and the languages spoken are Sinhala(the native language or the mother-tongue), Tamil, Malay and English.

About Animals

Based on the fact that islands do not have plenty of animals, Sri Lanka is quite the opposite when it comes to wildlife since even though its a small island, it packs great number of animals in the island within its vast national parks and zoos.

We currently nurture some rare animal species such as the :

  • Giant Panda
  • Leopard
  • Gorilla
  • Macaw
  • Giraffe
  • Sri Lankan Black Panther

About Plants and Trees

Sri Lanka is a country that packs a wide variety of trees and plants. The national Tree of Sri Lanka is the Mesua Ferrea (Scientific name) most commonly known as the " Na tree" amongst the locals and is also called "Ceylon Ironwood".

We currently nurture some endemic and endangered trees such as the :

  1. Hora Tree
  2. Na Tree
  3. Baobab Tree
  4. Walla-Patta Tree
